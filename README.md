# Phoenix Autocall Pricing : Monte Carlo under GBM and Heston

Monte Carlo pricing of a 2-Year USD Phoenix Autocall Note linked to the S&P 500® Index (ISIN XS2918085494, Morgan Stanley B.V.) under Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) and the Heston stochastic volatility model.

## Highlights

- 10,000,000 paths, daily time steps (Δt = 1/252), fully vectorised in NumPy.
- Memory coupon implemented per Phoenix market convention.
- Heston discretised with the Lord–Koekkoek–Van Dijk (2010) full-truncation scheme (Feller condition violated: 2κθ/ξ² = 0.48).
- Batched simulation reduces peak memory from ~40 GB to ~610 MB.
- Seeded reproducibility (NumPy PCG-64, seed = 42).

## Headline Results

| Model  | Fair Value (USD) | 95% CI           | Autocall Probability |
| ------ | ---------------- | ---------------- | -------------------- |
| GBM    | 998.36 ± 0.033   | [998.30, 998.43] | 77.31%               |
| Heston | 993.35 ± 0.040   | [993.27, 993.43] | 82.80%               |

Heston prices the note ~50 bps below GBM. The gap is driven by the fat left tail generated by ρ = −0.7: barrier breach scenarios are both more likely and more severe under stochastic volatility. The higher Heston autocall probability (via upside volatility compression) does not offset the tail loss.

Runtime: approximately 10 minutes on a modern CPU (GBM ~2 min, Heston ~7 min).

## Models

**GBM.** Exact log-normal discretisation under the risk-neutral measure with σ = 20% (matching √v₀ from Heston) and r = 4.0%. Zero discretisation bias.

**Heston.** Prescribed parameters v₀ = 0.04, θ = 0.04, κ = 1.5, ξ = 0.5, ρ = −0.7. Euler–Maruyama discretisation with full truncation of the variance process.
